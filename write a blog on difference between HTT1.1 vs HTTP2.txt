HTTP/1.1 has been the workhorse of the web since its standardization in 1999.
 It operates on a straightforward request-response model: a browser requests a resource (like a webpage or image) from a server, and the server sends it back. 
 While effective, HTTP/1.1 has its limitations.
 1.multilexing
 2. header overhead
 3. comression
 Recognizing the need for faster, more efficient web browsing experiences, HTTP/2 was developed and standardized in 2015. 
 It addresses many of the shortcomings of HTTP/1.1 through several key innovations
1.Multiplexing and Concurrency
2.Header Compression
3.Binary Protocol
